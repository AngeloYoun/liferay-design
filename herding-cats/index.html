Review effectively

these are some guidelines we have for our internal design reviews that we've found to be helpful when shared with others

Rules for Reviewing: 1/5

Set clear goals

  1. Improve the design
    • Is this useable?
    • Is it delightful?
  2. NOT:
    • Show everyone how smart you are
    • Make everyone happy

Rules for Reviewing: 2/5

Know where you are

Provide stage-appropriate feedback

while you're reviewing wireframes or user-flows, focus on the high-level things like...if I'm a user, will I know what will happen when I click this button ‚Äî NOT ‚Äî hey can we make this button darker?

Rules for Reviewing: 3/5

Provide problems,
not solutions

When getting feedback on a creative work, note that the solutions offered are usually wrong. But the problems identified are usually right.

— Neil Strauss (@neilstrauss) September 28, 2017
neil strauss is an author ‚Äî to be honest i'd never heard of him, but this tweet serendipitously appeared on my timeline during one of my breaks while preparing for this talk ‚Äî i thought it was quite fitting This is so true ‚Äî and why its important to help designers by pointing out problems, because then they don't have to work backwards and try to figure out...You can save a lot of wasted time by skipping the opinions and pointing out problems.

Rules for Reviewing: 4/5

Provide scenarios,
not opinions

this is another way we try to mitigate subjectivity - by using the third party, and prescribing the issue to a persona (or even a real person, if you have user recordings), it's an easy way to bring everyone's focus towards the users (of course, you'll have the people who use themself as a use-case...to which my go-to answer is "we can test that" ‚Äî because hey, maybe they're right)
